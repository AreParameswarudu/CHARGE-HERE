USE CASES

An electric vehicle (EV) charging station, also known as an EV charger or electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE), is a device that provides electric power to recharge the battery of an electric car or other plug-in electric vehicles. EV charging stations are becoming increasingly common as more people adopt electric vehicles. There are several types of EV charging stations, ranging from simple household outlets to high-powered commercial charging stations that can fully charge an electric vehicle in just a few hours. The most common types of EV charging stations include:

1.Level 1 Charging Station: This is the slowest type of charging station, providing 120 volts of alternating current (AC)power. It typically takes around 8-12 hours to fully charge an electric vehicle with a Level 1 charging station.

2.Level 2 Charging Station: This type of charging station provides 240 volts of AC power and is much faster than a Level1 station. A Level 2 charging station can fully charge an electric vehicle in 4-8 hours, depending on the size of the battery.

3.DC Fast Charging Station: This is the fastest type of EV charging station, providing direct current (DC) power to the vehicle's battery. DC fast charging stations can fully charge an electric vehicle in just 30 minutes to an hour.

EV charging stations can be found in many locations, including homes, workplaces, shopping centers, and along highways. Some charging stations are free, while others require payment, either through a subscription service or a pay-per-use system. As more electric vehicles hit the road, the demand for EV charging stations is expected to continue to grow, making EV charging station installation a promising business opportunity.

SINGLE-FAMILY

Home charging: This is the most common use case for EV charging. EV owners can charge their vehicles at home using a Level 1 or Level 2 charger. Level 1 chargers can be plugged into a standard 120V household outlet, while Level 2 chargers require a dedicated 240V circuit. Home charging is convenient and cost-effective for EV owners who have access to off-street parking.

MULTI-FAMILY

Multi-family EV charging refers to the installation of EV charging stations in a shared parking area or garage for use by multiple residents who own electric vehicles. It provides convenient access to charging for those who live in multi-unit residential properties, such as apartment buildings or condominiums. Multi-family EV charging stations are typically owned and managed by the building owner or property manager, and the cost is usually shared among the residents who use them. The installation of multi-family EV charging can help promote sustainability by encouraging the use of electric vehicles and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. The number of charging stations required is dependent on the number of electric vehicles owned by residents and their charging needs.

WORKPLACE

Employers can install EV chargers in their parking lots to encourage their employees to drive electric vehicles. This can help reduce emissions and promote sustainability while also providing a valuable employee benefit.

PUBLIC

Public charging stations are often located in parking lots, shopping centers, and other public areas to allow electric vehicle owners to recharge while they are away from home. These charging stations can provide a valuable service for those who do not have access to home charging or need to recharge while on the go.

OTHER USE CASES

FLEET-CHARGING

Companies that operate fleets of electric vehicles, such as delivery companies or taxi services, often install charging stations at their facilities to ensure that their vehicles are always fully charged and ready for use.

DESTINATION-CHARGING

EV chargers can be installed at hotels, restaurants, and other destination locations to provide a valuable service for electric vehicle owners. This can encourage visitors to choose these locations over others that do not offer EV charging.
